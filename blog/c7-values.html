Valuing Independence

2/1/2015

I find myself thinking a lot about how I look at my own indepence in the last few years. I moved to chicago around five years ago as big step towards indepence. Independence for myself, I had very few ties keeping me in the city I was in. I valued opportunity I had to take my life anywhere and I was floating on the wind a bit. After taking the first job in Chicago that hired me and seemed pretty decent, I quickly became pretty tied down to a job at a relatively small start up. Over the next few years of working there I relearned the value of my independence. Before when I had it, I didn't know what to do with it. My independence went away with a dead end job :(

The last time someone asked me for advice that comes to mind, it was of a romantic nature. Great romantic relationships foster independence. I look back on my relationships in the past and I have always felt supported in my personal decisions. A lack of independence can really put a wet blanket on a relationship. I think we all know this but when you're in a relationship it can be hard to see these things for yourself. As I said, independence has been a huge theme for me in the past few years, and in my last relationship as in my last job, there was a severe lack of it.

I feel very optimistic and I look forward towards the future when I think about my independence. I don't think that in the future I will suffer from a lack of independence as I have. This has been hard won ground. It took a long time to recognize that independence was something that I actually value, let alone something I need back in my life. I rearranged my life to reflect my new values. I parted ways with my last relationship and with the job I mentioned. I looked at the job market and it looked to me that web development would be the most accessible while hopefully providing a much higher degree of independence. As a good web developer, I'm likely to find a job in most cities around the US, and maybe even abroad. Or I could use my skills working at home, taking care of my dog and being a good dad to him :)
