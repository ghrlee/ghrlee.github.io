My attempt at explaining classes will hopefully be pretty fun because I think of this as a chance to get nerdy. Dungeons and dragons nerdy. In Dungeons and Dragons, we have the classic example of character classes. Think of the Dungeons and Dragons archetype classes: The Rogue, the Wizard, the Rastamon... and so on. Rastamon was in lord of the rings movie right? Anyway, what I'm going to attempt to explain is that any thing with an attribute or two, especially a quantifiable class, is a great opportunity for creating a class.

If we start to think about just the types of characters we listed above, we can see how their attributes set them apart. Our rogue is cunning, swift, good with a dagger, and probably has charm to spare. The wizard: wise, powerful, and oooooold. Rastamon?
